Kanda Bhaji Recipe

Onion pakora or Kanda bhaji, made with onions coated with besan (gram flour) and then deep-fried, is the most popular tea time snack recipe.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium onions thinly sliced
  • 1 cup gram flour or besan
  • 1 cup gram flour or besan
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 2 green chillies finely chopped
  • ½ cup fresh coriander leaves finely chopped
  • ½ tsp haldi/turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp lal mirch/red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp kitchen king masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Pinch of asafoetida
  • 1 tsp ajwain/carom seeds roughly crushed
  • 1 tsp jeera/cumin seeds roughly crushed

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add besan (gram flour) and rice flour.
  2. Then, add jeera, ajwain, haldi, red chilli powder, kitchen king masala, asafoetida, and salt into the same bowl.
  3. Give it a nice mix and then add finely chopped green chillies, coriander leaves and thinly sliced onions.
  4. Now, add a tsp of oil and without adding water mix all the dry ingredients with onion. And while mixing squeeze onions with little pressure, it will release the moisture from onions.
  5. Then, add little water in the bowl and get a proper consistency. For soft and dense pakoras, add little amount of water. And For crispy and crunchy pakoras, add more water till onions can be held together.
  6. Now, cover and keep the batter aside for 15 to 20 mins.
  7. Meanwhile, heat the oil in the pan for frying on medium-high flame.
  8. Now, mix the besan and onion batter with the spoon or fork and put a small drop of besan in oil to check if oil is sufficiently hot.
  9. When the oil is sufficiently hot, drop the spoonful of onions in the oil and keep the flame on medium.
  10. Depending on the size of kadhai, drop few more onion batter in the pan and accordingly lower or increase the flame.
  11. When the pakoras are fried from one side, flip it with the help of a slotted spoon.
  12. Fry the pakoras from both the sides by flipping it until they are evenly cooked and turns crisp and golden brown in colour.
  13. Now, remove the fried pakoras with the slotted spoon and drain the excess oil and place it on a kitchen towel.
  14. Similarly, fry the remaining onion pakoras in the same way.
